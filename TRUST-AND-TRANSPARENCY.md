# Trust and Transparency

This document explains what SendSwap is, what it does, and what it does not claim to do.

## Service model

SendSwap is a non-custodial instant crypto exchange interface for wallet-to-wallet swaps.

In normal use:

- users do not create an account on SendSwap
- users do not deposit into a custodial balance
- users send from their own wallet
- users receive directly into their own wallet

## Supported use case

SendSwap is designed for simple cross-asset crypto swaps such as:

- BTC to XMR
- XMR to USDT
- ETH to BTC
- SOL to XMR

The platform supports 2000+ assets and offers fixed and floating rate routes, depending on availability.

## What SendSwap does not claim

SendSwap does not claim to be:

- a custodial exchange with user accounts and balances
- an order-book trading venue
- a wallet provider that holds customer keys
- a guarantee that no downstream compliance or risk checks can ever happen in the wider execution path

## KYC boundary

For standard swaps on SendSwap, users are not required to create an account or complete KYC on the SendSwap platform itself.

At the same time, some swap routes may depend on third-party execution or liquidity infrastructure. In those cases, operational, compliance, or risk-related checks may be applied outside SendSwap's direct control.

## Fees and rates

SendSwap displays the quote and applicable fee information before confirmation. Rate mode may be fixed or floating depending on route selection and market conditions.
